    Factorial (Combinatorics)

    The factorial of a non-negative integer $n$, denoted $n!$, is the product of all positive integers from 1 to $n$. The Factorial of a number $n$ is the number of Permutations of $n$ distinct elements.

    • Classical Representation: The product of all integers from $n$ down to 1.
      • $n!=\begin{cases}1&\text{if } n=0 \\ n \cdot (n-1) \cdot (n-2) \cdot \ldots \cdot2 \cdot 1 & \text{if } n > 0\end{cases}$
    • Representation with Product Notation: More formally:
      • $n!=\begin{cases}1&\text{if } n=0 \\ \prod\limits_{k=1}^{n}k & \text{if } n > 0\end{cases}$
    • Factorial of Zero: The factorial of zero is one.
      • $0!=1$
    • Examples
      • $5!=5\cdot4\cdot3\cdot2\cdot1=120$
      • $7!=7\cdot6\cdot5\cdot4\cdot3\cdot2\cdot1=5040$
    • Example of simplification with factorial fractions:
      • $\cfrac{6!}{4!}=\cfrac{6\cdot5}{4}=\cfrac{6\cdot5\cdot\cancel{4\cdot3\cdot2}}{4\cdot\cancel{3\cdot2}}$
