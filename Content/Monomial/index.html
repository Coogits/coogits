    Monomial

    Monomial is a Polynomial with exactly one term. In other words, a monomial is a specific case of a polynomial in which the degree (or the number of terms) is equal to 1.

    • Key Points:
      • A monomial has only one term.
      • The exponents of the variables must be non-negative integers.

    Symbols

    • $a\quad$ Constant coefficient: It is a Real Number.
    • $x_0,x_1,\ldots,x_n\quad$ Variables: They are a indeterminate values.
    • $k_0,k_1,\ldots,k_n\quad$ Exponents: They are non-negative Integer Numbers, the sum of them represent the degree of the monomial.

    Notation

    • Generic Form:
      In general can be represented as: $$a\cdot x_0^{k_0}\cdot x_1^{k_1}\cdot\ldots\cdot x_n^{k_n}$$
    • Standard Form:
      Is a monomial expressed with ordered letters: Example: $$axy^2z\ldots$$

    Degree of a Monomial

    Degree of a Monomial Refers to the highest power of the variable (or the sum of the exponents of the variables) in the monomial. It represents the total degree of the term.

    • Degree of a Monomial:
      In the generic form above, the degree of the monomial $a\cdot x_0^{k_0}\cdot x_1^{k_1}\cdot\ldots\cdot x_n^{k_n}$ is: $$k_0+k_1+\ldots+k_n$$

    Examples

    • ✔️ Example 1
      • $7x^5y^2z$
    • ❌ Example 2:
      this is not a monomial
      • ${\color{gray} x+y}$
    • Monomial's Degree Examples
      • $7x^5y^2z \quad\to\quad 5+2+1=8$
