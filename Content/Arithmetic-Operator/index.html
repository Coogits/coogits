    Arithmetic Operator

    An arithmetic operator is a fundamental mathematical symbol or function that represents a specific operation to manipulate numbers. These operations are the building blocks of mathematics, enabling calculations on numerical values, often drawn from different sets and the Numeral System.

    PEDMAS

    Conventional order of Operations: The standard hierarchy of operations in mathematics, often abbreviated as PEMDAS (Parentheses, Exponents, Multiplication/Division, Addition/Subtraction), is as follows:

    1. Parenthesis:
      Note: Inner grouping symbols take precedence over outer ones if they are nested.
      1. Curly Braces: $\{\;\}$.
      2. Squared Brackets: $[\;]$.
      3. Round Braces: $(\;)$.
    2. Exponentiation:
      Perform power, squares, logarithm.
      1. $a^2;\; \sqrt{a};\; \log{x};\;\ln{x}$.
    3. Multiplication and Division:
      Perform from left to right.
      1. $a\cdot b;\;\cfrac{a}{b}$.
    4. Addition and Subtraction:
      Perform from left to right.
      1. $a\pm b$.
    5. Logical Operations (Booleans):
      In computer science.
      1. OR, AND, XOR, ....
