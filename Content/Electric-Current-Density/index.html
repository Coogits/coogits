    Electric Current Density

    Electric current density (usually denoted by $\mathbf{J}$ or simply $J$) is a measure of the electric current flowing per unit area through a cross-section of a conductor. It is a vector quantity that indicates both the magnitude of current per unit area and the direction in which the positive charges (or conventional current) are flowing.

    Symbols

    • $J\quad$ Electric Current Density (unit: Ampere per square meter ($\frac{A}{m^2}$))
    • $I\quad$ Electric Current (unit: Ampere (A))
    • $E\quad$ Electric Field (unit: Newton per Coulomb, $\frac{N}{C}$ or Volts per meter $\left(\frac{V}{M}\right)$ )
    • $\sigma\quad$ Electric Conductivity (unit: Siemens per meters ($\frac{S}{m}$))
    • $A\quad$ Cross-Sectional Area (unit: square meters $(m^2)$).
    • $\eta\quad$ Number Density of Charge Carriers (unit: $m^{-3}$)
    • $e\quad$ Elementary Charge (unit: Coulombs (C))
    • $\vec v\quad$ Drift Velocity of the charge carriers (unit: meters per second $\left(\frac{m}{s}\right)$)

    Formulas

    • Uniform Current Distribution:
      When the current is uniformly distributed over a cross-sectional area $A$, the current density is given by: $$J=\cfrac{I}{A}$$
    • General (Differential) Form:
      For a non-uniform distribution, the current density is defined as the differential amount of current $dI$ per differential area $dA$: $$J=\frac{dI}{dA}$$
    • Relationship with Electric Field:
      In a conductive material, the current density is often related to the electric field $E$ through the material's conductivity $\sigma$ by the differential form of Ohm's law: $$J=\sigma E$$
    • Microscopic Relationship:
      The electric current density can also be expressed in terms of the properties of the charge carriers: $$\vec{J}=\eta e\vec{v}_d$$

    Units

    • SI Unit:
      The SI unit for electric current density is Ampere per square meter: $$\frac{A}{m^2}$$
