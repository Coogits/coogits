    Multiplication (Polynomial)

    Polynomial Multiplication Is a operation between two polynomials involves multiplying each term of the first polynomial by each term of the second polynomial, and then summing the resulting partial products. Afterward, the resulting polynomial is simplified, if necessary, into its standard form, where like terms are combined and the terms are ordered by decreasing exponents.

    Notation

    • Notation with symbols:
      A multiplication between Polynomial is mostly expressed: $$P(x)\cdot Q(x)$$ Expanding the polynomials: $$(a_1x_1+b_1\ldots)\cdot(a_2x_2+b_2\ldots)$$

    Multiplication Steps

    Algorithm

    1. Standard Form:
      If necessary, express the polynomials in their Standard Form (Algebra).
    2. Partial Products:
      Calculate the partial products by multiplying each term of the first polynomial with each term of the second polynomial.
    3. Sum:
      Sum the partial products.
    4. Simplify:
      If necessary, simplify the resulting polynomial into its standard form.
