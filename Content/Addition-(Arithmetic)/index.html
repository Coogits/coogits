    Addition

    Addition is the arithmetical operation of combining two or more numbers to obtain their total or sum. It is denoted by the plus sign +.

    Symbols

    • $a,b\quad$ Addends: the numbers or values being added (e.g. 1, 2, 45, variable...).
    • $c\quad$ Sum: the result of the addition.
    • $i\quad$ Index: variable used in summation to iterate through terms.
    • $\sum\quad$ Summation (Arithmetic)
    • $i=0\quad$ Starting index in summation.
    • $n\quad$ End index in summation.

    Notation

    • Basic Form: Addition of two numbers is expressed as: $$a+b=c$$
    • Summation:
      Represents the addition of multiple terms. $$\displaystyle\sum_{i=0}^na_i=a_0+a_1+\ldots+a_n$$

    Properties

    • Commutative:
      The order of addition does not change the sum: $$a+b=b+a$$
    • Associative Property:
      Grouping does not affect the sum: $$(a+b)+c=a+(b+c)$$
    • Identity Element:
      Adding zero to any number leaves it unchanged: $$a+0=a$$
    • Parity Rule (Even/Odd Sum Property):
      • The sum of two even numbers is even.
      • The sum of two odd numbers is even.
      • The sum of an even and an odd number is odd.
