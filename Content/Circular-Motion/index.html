    Circular Motion

    Definition of Circular Motion

    Circular motion is the movement of an object along a circular path. This motion can occur at a constant speed (uniform circular motion) or with a changing speed (non-uniform circular motion).

    Types of Circular Motion:

    1. Uniform Circular Motion (UCM):

      • The object moves in a circle with constant speed.
      • The velocity changes direction but maintains the same magnitude.
      • The centripetal acceleration directed toward the center.

    2. Non-Uniform Circular Motion:

      • The object's speed varies along the circular path.
      • There is an additional tangential acceleration component due to speed changes.
      • The total acceleration has both centripetal and tangential components.
