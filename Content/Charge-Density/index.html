    Charge Density

    Charge density describes how electric charge is distributed over a given dimension (length, surface, or volume). It quantifies the amount of charge per unit of space in different geometrical configurations.

    Types

    • Linear Charge Density $\lambda$:
      The amount of charge per unit length, typically used for charged wires or filaments. $$\lambda=\cfrac{Q}{L}$$
    • Surface Charge Density $\sigma$:
      The amount of charge per unit length, typically used for charged wires or filaments. $$\sigma=\cfrac{Q}{S}$$
    • Volume Charge Density $\sigma$:
      The amount of charge per unit length, typically used for charged wires or filaments. $$\rho=\cfrac{Q}{V}$$

    Units

    • Linear Charge Density SI Unit $\lambda$: $$\cfrac{C}{m}$$
    • Surface Charge Density $\sigma$: $$\cfrac{C}{m^2}$$
    • Volume Charge Density $\sigma$: $$\cfrac{C}{m^3}$$
