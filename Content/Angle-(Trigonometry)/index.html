    Angle (Trigonometry)

    An angle represents the rotation between two intersecting lines or rays, typically measured in degrees or radians. It is a key concept used to describe the relationships between the sides and angles of triangles, particularly right-angled triangles, and is fundamental in defining trigonometric functions such as sine, cosine, and tangent.

    Symbols

    • $\alpha\quad$ Represents the angle (in radians), often denoted by $\theta$ as well.
    • $\alpha^\circ\quad$ Represents the angle in degrees, also commonly written as $\theta^\circ$.
    • $\tan(\alpha)\quad$ Represents the tangent of the angle.
    • $\sin(\alpha)\quad$ sine of the angle. It is the y-coordinate of a point on the unit circle corresponding to the angle, which is the ratio of the opposite side to the hypotenuse in a right-angled triangle.
    • $\sin(\alpha)\quad$ Represents the cosine of the angle. It is the x-coordinate of a point on the unit circle corresponding to the angle, which is the ratio of the adjacent side to the hypotenuse in a right-angled triangle.

    Formulas

    • Angle Units:
      Degree-Radian conversion: $$\alpha:\alpha^\circ =\pi:180^\circ$$
    • Angle Inverse Functions:
      From the Trigonometric Function: $$\alpha=\arctan(\tan(\alpha)) \qquad \alpha\in\left[-\cfrac{\pi}{2},\cfrac{\pi}{2}\right]$$$$\alpha=\arcsin(\sin(\alpha)) \qquad \alpha\in[0,\pi]$$$$\alpha=\arccos(\cos(\alpha)) \qquad \alpha\in\left(-\cfrac{\pi}{2},\cfrac{\pi}{2}\right)$$
